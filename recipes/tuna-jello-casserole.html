Ingridients

  • 2 cans (about 10 ounces each) tuna, drained and flaked
  • 1 cup mixed veggies (e.g., peas carrots, corn), thawed if frozen
  • 1/2 cup diced celery
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped onion
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 2 cups boiling water
  • 1 packet (about 0.25 ounces) unflavored gelatin
  • 1/4 cup cold water
  • 1 cup cold water for chilling the gelatin
  • Fresh herbs for garnish (optional)
Instructions
  1. Prepare Vegetables:
    In a bowl, combine the drained and flaked tuna with mixed vegetables, diced celery, and finely chopped onion.
  2. Make Tuna Mixtures:
    In a separate mixing bowl, whisk together mayonnaise, lemon juice, Dijon mustard, salt, and black pepper. Add the tuna and vegetable mixture to the bowl with the mayo mixture. Mix until well combined. Set aside.
  3. Bloom Gelatin:
    In a small bowl, sprinkle the unflavored gelatin over 1/4 cup of cold water. Allow it to sit for a few minutes to bloom.
  4. Dissolve Gelatin:
    Pour 2 cups of boiling water over the bloomed gelatin, stirring until completely dissolved.
  5. Combine Tuna Mixture and Gelatin:
    Gradually pour the dissolved gelatin mixture into the tuna and vegetable mixture, stirring continuously to ensure even distribution.
  6. Chill:
    Pour the combined mixture into a casserole dish. Refrigerate for at least 4 hours or until the gelatin is fully set.
  7. Serve:
    Once set, cut the casserole into squares or desired shapes. Garnish with fresh herbs if desired. Serve chilled and enjoy your unique Tuna Veggie Jello Casserole!
